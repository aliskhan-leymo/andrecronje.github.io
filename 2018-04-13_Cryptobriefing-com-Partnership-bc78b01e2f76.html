I started doing code reviews because they were a mechanism for me to teach myself. During this process, unfortunately, I learned of a lot of projects that are just taking advantage of the current state of affairs in the crypto space.

This led me to start sharing my reviews and data. At the core of it, my passion is still just development, but at the same time, there was a demand for sharing this data. Originally I thought, there wouldn’t be any interest, what does a code review really matter? And to tell you the truth, in the short term, I don’t think they do. I have seen no correlation between short term speculation and code quality. Long term on the other hand, is a very different story.

With that in mind Han Kao (https://www.linkedin.com/in/hankao) /of cryptobriefing.com reached out and we began a few discussions. They had seen a similar pattern in this industry and also had a shared goal of helping share valuable data and insight to better inform the crypto enthusiasts.

Originally I was skeptical, one thing that remained important to me, was that this service had to be free and uncensored. I have been adamant on not accepting payments, or even donations, for any work. At no time do I want it to reflect on a review. Han however shared this passion, and their own model is one of open, and transparent data share.

With the shared vision and shared philosophy we decided to band together, they bring the fundamental, and statistical technical analysis side, I bring the code analysis. So, I’m very happy to announce our partnership and that from now on cryptobriefing.com will be my primary platform for reviews.

I look forward to still sharing as much data as possible in a free, transparent, and open platform!
