“ It is a Blockchain Platform Technology for Edenchain’s Implementation of Programmable Economy. Through Edenchain, capitalize tangible/ intangible values, and trade it safely, cost efficiently, and fast It could be used easily by company/organization/individual who need a blockchain technology and coin”

Another permissioned blockchain. Let us talk for a moment about permissioned blockchains. So if we look at Ethereum or Bitcoin (or any other public network), we have a very (theoretically) even distribution of wealth. Any participating node has an equal chance to win the reward. This means that the rewards aren’t monopolized and spread amongst a lot of different members. This is important since this is what is needed for a digital economy to survive. If 1 person holds 50% of the economy, that is not a stable economy, 50% of it is stable, but that other 50% essentially controls it.

So why am I so against permissioned blockchains? Because why have a blockchain at all. The amazing thing about blockchain specifically is that it is cryptographically secure and open for public consumption. This means we don’t have to trust any one entity. On a permissioned blockchain, you are trusting the validators. This means you don’t need to use blockchain. They might as well just have a public instance of cassandra/mongodb/other running and give read only rights to the entire public. The second you see “permissioned” and “blockchain” together, you have to ask, then why have a blockchain? Blockchain as a technology is just an immutable concatenated list, that’s all. So if you are “permissioned” you don’t need blockchain, use any of the other data storage options available that are a thousand times better at processing and throughput. Blockchain isn’t the amazing invention, having a tamper proof shared ledger is the amazing thing, the second you say it’s permissioned you take away all it’s power.

And then we aren’t even talking yet about the token economics, because remember, every transaction fee, every “block” that gets mined is awarded to that validator. What’s it called again when all the money goes to the same entities over and over again? Oh right, a monopoly!

Sigh. All of this really just makes me sad. We have open BFT, we have PoS, you can even do completely random, but instead, every other “blockchain” is coming out as permissioned, why? Because with permissioned you can claim you are fixing security and scalability issues. Guess what, you aren’t, you are just throwing away blockchain and instead saying you might as well just host a database on your servers, you can trust us.
