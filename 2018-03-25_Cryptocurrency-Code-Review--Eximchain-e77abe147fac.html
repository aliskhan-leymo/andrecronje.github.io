Disclaimer: These reviews are done as is from what is on display in the master branch of the repo’s made available. This review is not a comment on the overall project, scope, or success thereof. This was done as an educational review by me and any comments in the article are simply my opinion. It should not be used as any comment or advice on the project as a whole.

EDIT 29/03/2018 Feedback from EximChain

Hi Andre,

Thanks for taking the time to review our code, our large scale network testing results are available on https://github.com/Eximchain/quorum-stress-tests. Our current testnet has around 250 machines running the protocol on real, not simulated, infrastructure live in US, Canada, Brazil, England, Ireland, Germany, France, Singapore, Japan, Australia, India; automated and managed through Infrastructure as Code. With Infra as Code we will offer our users to manage distributed blockchain, api’s, and key management infrastructures to support their applications. We will release our 10 part video tutorial series on how to use Eximchain’s self-built tools to develop decentralized enterprise infrastructure on AWS soon, hopefully you will consider doing a review again:)

Regards,

Eximchain

Me: So their goal right now is infrastructure, and they are focusing on it a lot (as you will see from their terraformer application as well), so right now they are less focused on the actual blockchain code, but instead on creating an easy, sustainable, enterprise grade solution of infrastructure to build on top of. This is how you build your foundation the right way. I actually really hope they explore this as a solution and offer it as a service to other blockchain companies as well. I love how each team is focusing on a different angle and it shows a really positive combined future.
